



Max Green Skill Development Organization — History (2010–2025)


Founded: 2010

Type: Non-profit; education & skills (formal, non-formal, vocational, social welfare)

Core Areas (Sindh): Karachi, Mirpurkhas, Umerkot, Tharparkar, Jamshoro (and nearby districts)

Key Partners (selected): Sindh Education Foundation (SEF), Directorate of Literacy & Non-Formal Education (SELD), BBSHRRDB, NAVTTC, STEVTA, Trade Testing Board

Flagship NFE Center: Makhdoom Bilawal (Karachi) for underprivileged learners (approx. ages 9–20)

Academic Structure: Package A (Grades 1–2), Package B (Grades 2–3), Package C (Grades 4–5) with curriculum in English/Urdu/Math; phonics-based English with CVC/short/long vowels & sentence building

Phase-wise Timeline
2010–2014: Foundation & Early Outreach

MGSDO established with a mission to empower communities through education, skills, and social welfare.

Initial literacy and community classes begin in Karachi’s underserved neighborhoods.

Start of non-formal education (NFE) pilots; early coordination with local volunteers and teachers.

2015–2018: Program Expansion & Partnerships

NFE classes scale to additional localities (e.g., Mirpurkhas, Umerkot, Tharparkar).

Partnership building with provincial/skill bodies (e.g., SEF, Directorate of Literacy & NFE, BBSHRRDB, NAVTTC, STEVTA).

Start formalizing teacher appointment letters, collaboration agreements, and testing/certification pathways via public boards.

Community welfare add-ons: parent meetings, awareness sessions, and basic livelihoods support.

2019–2021: Systems, Curriculum & Certification Pathways

Curriculum structuring for Package A/B/C with weekly → daily lesson planning, phonics and foundational numeracy.

Focus on teacher methodology, classroom resources, and simple M&E (monitoring & evaluation) routines.

Learner documentation improves (admissions, exam schedules, letters to departments like NADRA for age corrections, etc.).

Strengthened links to testing & trade boards for vocational tracks.

2022–2024: Digital Presence & Operational Documentation

Significant work on digital identity and multi-site deployment (Netlify sub-sites, Hostinger, custom domain).

Purchase & setup of maxgreenskills.org; experimenting with DNS (Cloudflare), subdomains, and site hosting.

Creation of official letters, agreements, forms, staff/infrastructure tables, and academic calendars/syllabi.

Roll-out of assessment timetables, academic support plans, and refined lesson planning (objectives, activities, resources, evaluation).

Exploration of AI/tech for ops: prompts, chat assistants, and lightweight web apps.

2025 (to Nov 2, 2025): Consolidation, Visibility & SEF Engagement

Continued website work (multi-page responsive templates; social/media sections; WhatsApp quick-contact).

Stakeholder engagement:

Meetings highlighted with SEF Karachi Region leadership (e.g., Mr. Kashif—District Head; Mr. Ashfaq Mirani—Regional Head) to deepen collaboration for quality education and skills.

Ongoing refinement of the NFE center at Makhdoom Bilawal (9–20 age group); emphasis on foundational literacy/phonics, basic math, and skill development orientation.

Public-facing communication improved (news/events posts, gallery, and partnership updates).

Continued work on School Establishment Plan for SEF and long-term formal school expansion roadmap (5–15 year lens).

Program Pillars (Current)

Non-Formal Education (NFE): Accelerated literacy & bridging for out-of-school youth (Packages A–C).

Formal Education (Roadmap): Step-wise plan to expand grades and stabilize school operations with SEF alignment.

Skill Development: Linkages to NAVTTC/STEVTA/Trade Testing Board for vocational pathways (dressmaking, IT assistant, beauty therapy, Python/AI basics, etc.).

Social Welfare & Community Work: Parent engagement, counseling, dropout mitigation, and support drives.

Digital & AI Enablement: Websites, content, basic AI tools for instruction/support, and structured documentation workflows.

Governance & Operations (Ongoing Practices)

Documentation: Teacher appointment letters, collaboration MoUs, bank letters for facilitators, exam schedules, and fee/security correspondences (e.g., BBSHRRDB refunds).

Academic Quality: Weekly → daily lesson plans; smart objectives; assessments; monitoring visits; parent meetings.

Compliance: Coordination with SEF/Directorate for NFE compliance; ID/age corrections via relevant departments.

Visibility: Social media, news & events, and photo galleries to showcase progress and partnerships.

Selected 2025 Highlights (Jan–Nov 2, 2025)

Website/domain & DNS troubleshooting and improvements (Cloudflare ↔ Hostinger ↔ Netlify flows).

Content upgrades: About, Programs (Formal/NFE/Curriculum), Skill Development, Social Activities, Research, Media, Team, and AI voice assistant exploration on the site.

SEF engagement meetings (Kashif/Ashfaq Mirani) emphasizing collaboration for quality and scale.

Refinement of English phonics curriculum and daily lesson flow; continued use of Packages A/B/C.

Looking Ahead (2026 Roadmap — High Level)

Stabilize one flagship formal campus aligned with SEF standards while scaling NFE satellites.

Strengthen teacher training (phonics, classroom management, remedial pathways).

Formalize vocational tracks with clear certification & placement guidance.

Complete monitoring & evaluation dashboards; light data collection for outcomes.

Mature the digital platform (content, admissions interest forms, volunteer/donor onboarding, AI assistant for FAQs).
